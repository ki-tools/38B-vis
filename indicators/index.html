Geo-temporal views of STI-related indicators
The links below will take you to interactive geo-temporal views of data for each specified indicator. The data comes from the DHS StatCompiler, with the temporal data, geographic data, and geograhpic shapefiles all needing to be manually merged. Note that survey regions can change over time so there will be some discontinuities with that regard. Unfortunately data coming from this system does not provide confidence intervals. We can compute confidence intervals and have better geographic/temporal matching integrity using the individual-level DHS/IPUMS data, but have not yet been able to find all of the indicators we need from that source.
  • Median age at first sexual intercourse
  • Young women age 15-19 who ever had sexual intercourse
  • Young women age 15-19 who are sexually active
  • Young men age 15-19 who ever had sexual intercourse
  • Young men age 15-19 who are sexually active
  • Men circumcised
  • Women reporting an STI
  • Women reporting an STI, genital dicharge, or a sore or ulcer
  • Men reporting an STI
  • Men reporting an STI, genital dicharge, or a sore or ulcer
  • HIV prevalence among women
  • HIV prevalence among men
The visualizations on the left show the historical trend of the indicator prevelance at each subnational region while the linked map on the right shows the prevalence for the latest available year for each given subnational region. The temporal visualizations help provide context about the trajectory of the indicator of interest, while the sub-national geographical view helps understand geographic heterogeneity. The information presented in this way can be helpful in assisting with qualitative decisions after having been provided with recommendations based on quantitative methods.
